<section class="product-problem" aria-labelledby="product-problem-title">
  <div class="studio-shell product-editorial-grid">
    <div class="product-editorial-grid__lead reveal">
      <p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.background_label">Background / Design hypothesis</p>
      <h2 id="product-problem-title" data-i18n="product.background_title">Charging products solve power, but often create a new layer of desk friction.</h2>
      <p data-i18n="product.background_text">This self-initiated study starts from observable everyday conditions rather than claimed user research: loose cables, uncertain alignment, fixed charging parts, and accessories that compete visually with the device.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="problem-list reveal">
      <article><span>01</span><h3 data-i18n="product.problem_1_title">Scattered setup</h3><p data-i18n="product.problem_1_text">A dock, puck, and cable often behave as separate objects.</p></article>
      <article><span>02</span><h3 data-i18n="product.problem_2_title">Unclear placement</h3><p data-i18n="product.problem_2_text">The user must search for the correct charging position.</p></article>
      <article><span>03</span><h3 data-i18n="product.problem_3_title">Hidden cable path</h3><p data-i18n="product.problem_3_text">Cable routing is treated as an afterthought.</p></article>
      <article><span>04</span><h3 data-i18n="product.problem_4_title">Single-use hardware</h3><p data-i18n="product.problem_4_text">A fixed charging component cannot move with the user.</p></article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="studio-section product-strategy" aria-labelledby="product-strategy-title">
  <div class="studio-shell">
    <header class="section-heading section-heading--split reveal">
      <p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.strategy_label">Design strategy</p>
      <h2 id="product-strategy-title" data-i18n="product.strategy_title">Make every function readable before it is explained.</h2>
    </header>
    <div class="strategy-principles">
      <article class="reveal"><span>01</span><h3 data-i18n="product.principle_1_title">One dominant gesture</h3><p data-i18n="product.principle_1_text">A low base and one rising arc establish the product silhouette.</p></article>
      <article class="reveal"><span>02</span><h3 data-i18n="product.principle_2_title">Visible functional zones</h3><p data-i18n="product.principle_2_text">Dock, puck, and cable route remain visually distinct.</p></article>
      <article class="reveal"><span>03</span><h3 data-i18n="product.principle_3_title">Calm desk presence</h3><p data-i18n="product.principle_3_text">The product stays below the visual weight of the device.</p></article>
      <article class="reveal"><span>04</span><h3 data-i18n="product.principle_4_title">Modular service logic</h3><p data-i18n="product.principle_4_text">The power element and cable carrier are conceived as separable parts.</p></article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="product-visual-section" aria-labelledby="product-concept-title">
  <div class="studio-shell">
    <header class="product-visual-section__header reveal">
      <div><p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.concept_label">Concept &amp; form development</p><h2 id="product-concept-title" data-i18n="product.concept_title">From object on the desk to part of the desk.</h2></div>
      <p data-i18n="product.concept_text">Three digital massing directions were compared through visual weight, access, functional hierarchy, and silhouette. The selected ARC direction reduces height while keeping each interaction visible.</p>
    </header>
    <figure class="product-visual reveal"><img src="{{ '/assets/images/product-design/arc-dock-evolution.svg' | relative_url }}" alt="ARC Dock product form evolution across three concepts" width="1600" height="900" loading="lazy"></figure>

</section>

<section class="product-visual-section product-visual-section--dark" aria-labelledby="product-interaction-title">
  <div class="studio-shell">
    <header class="product-visual-section__header reveal">
      <div><p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.interaction_label">Function &amp; interaction</p><h2 id="product-interaction-title" data-i18n="product.interaction_title">Three gestures organize the experience.</h2></div>
      <p data-i18n="product.interaction_text">Place the device, lift the puck when power needs to move, and route the cable through an exposed path. No hidden mode or decorative interaction is added.</p>
    </header>
    <figure class="product-visual reveal"><img src="{{ '/assets/images/product-design/arc-dock-interaction.svg' | relative_url }}" alt="ARC Dock docking, charging puck, and cable routing interaction sequence" width="1600" height="900" loading="lazy"></figure>

</section>

<section class="product-split-section" aria-labelledby="product-cmf-title">
  <div class="studio-shell product-split-section__grid">
    <div class="product-split-section__copy reveal">
      <p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.cmf_label">CMF design</p>
      <h2 id="product-cmf-title" data-i18n="product.cmf_title">Material contrast clarifies touch, structure, and brand character.</h2>
      <p data-i18n="product.cmf_text">The proposed CMF system separates the structural shell, soft contact areas, and charging interface. Graphite is the primary direction; silver and studio purple demonstrate how the same form can support different brand positions.</p>
      <small data-i18n="product.cmf_note">Material and finish selections are concept proposals and require engineering validation.</small>
    </div>
    <figure class="product-split-section__visual reveal"><img src="{{ '/assets/images/product-design/arc-dock-cmf.svg' | relative_url }}" alt="ARC Dock graphite, silver, and purple CMF proposals" width="1600" height="900" loading="lazy"></figure>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="product-split-section product-split-section--reverse" aria-labelledby="product-structure-title">
  <div class="studio-shell product-split-section__grid">
    <figure class="product-split-section__visual reveal"><img src="{{ '/assets/images/product-design/arc-dock-exploded.svg' | relative_url }}" alt="ARC Dock conceptual exploded structure and components" width="1600" height="1000" loading="lazy"></figure>
    <div class="product-split-section__copy reveal">
      <p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.structure_label">Structure &amp; detail</p>
      <h2 id="product-structure-title" data-i18n="product.structure_title">A layered assembly keeps the visual shell separate from functional carriers.</h2>
      <p data-i18n="product.structure_text">The concept separates an outer shell, internal carrier, angled support, removable puck, cable cassette, and non-slip foot. This is a design architecture, not an engineering specification.</p>
      <small data-i18n="product.structure_note">Fasteners, electronics, tolerances, thermal behavior, and manufacturing feasibility remain outside this concept scope.</small>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="studio-section product-iteration" aria-labelledby="product-iteration-title">
  <div class="studio-shell">
    <header class="section-heading section-heading--split reveal">
      <p class="studio-eyebrow" data-i18n="product.iteration_label">Prototype &amp; iteration</p>
      <h2 id="product-iteration-title" data-i18n="product.iteration_title">Digital proportion studies exposed the decisions that mattered.</h2>
    </header>
    <ol class="iteration-timeline">
      <li class="reveal"><span>01</span><div><h3 data-i18n="product.iteration_1_title">Reduce vertical dominance</h3><p data-i18n="product.iteration_1_text">The tower direction saved area but competed visually with the device.</p></div></li>
      <li class="reveal"><span>02</span><div><h3 data-i18n="product.iteration_2_title">Separate functional zones</h3><p data-i18n="product.iteration_2_text">The bridge direction clarified use but left unnecessary rear mass.</p></div></li>
      <li class="reveal"><span>03</span><div><h3 data-i18n="product.iteration_3_title">Open the silhouette</h3><p data-i18n="product.iteration_3_text">The ARC direction lowered the base, exposed cable access, and made the puck legible.</p></div></li>
    </ol>
  </div>
</section>
